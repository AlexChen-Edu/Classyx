My Brother Says He Studied But I Don't Buy It Anymore

Figured out what was actually going on after a while

My brother is 15 and failing math. Not like struggling a little — actually failing. And every single night he goes to his room, closes the door, and comes out an hour later saying he's done.

I stopped believing him after the third test in a row came back with a 40-something on it.

The thing is, I don't think he's straight up lying. He probably does open his textbook. He probably looks at it for like ten minutes. But then his phone is right there, YouTube is right there, and honestly math is hard and confusing and it's way easier to just close the book and tell yourself you tried.

And I had no idea any of this was happening because I trusted him to handle it. He's 15, not 8. You can't sit next to him and watch him do homework.

But you also can't just hope for the best when the grades are showing you it's not working.

The part that got me

What really got me was realizing I had zero visibility into what was actually going on. Like none. I knew he was in his room. I knew he said he studied. That was it.

By the time the report card came I'd missed weeks of him falling further and further behind. And at that point catching up feels impossible so he just... doesn't try.

If I'd known earlier — even just known he was only spending 10 minutes a night on it instead of an hour — I could've stepped in before it got bad.

What I'm trying now

I found something called Classyx that I'm actually pretty excited to try with him. He'd take a photo of his notes and it turns them into flashcards automatically — which honestly sounds way more likely to get him to actually study than staring at a textbook.

But the part that got me was the parent dashboard. You can see when they're studying and for how long. Not hovering over them, just enough to know if they put in real time that day or not.

That's exactly what I needed. Just enough visibility to know whether to check in or leave it alone. We're trying it this week. If you're dealing with the same thing, you can sign up for early access at getclassyx.com.
